## 2. The Will: Discrete Graph Search

### 2.1 The Optimization Objective

The responsibility of the Will is to navigate the Cayley Graph to find a node $S^*$ such that the projected logical path satisfies zero energy. 

To reconcile the discrete nature of logic with the need for search guidance, we use two maps:

1.  **Materialization Map ($\Pi$):** $Cl(\Delta) \to \text{ProofAction}^*$ (Discrete output)
2.  **Feature Map ($\Psi$):** $Cl(\Delta) \to \mathbb{R}^k$ (Continuous embedding)

**Optimization Goal:**

$$\text{Minimize } J(S) = E_{barrier}(\Pi(S)) + E_{residual}(\Psi(S))$$
$$\text{subject to } S \in V(\mathcal{G})$$

### 2.2 VAPO on Graphs

Since gradients $\nabla J$ are undefined on a discrete graph, **VAPO (Valuation-Adaptive Perturbation Optimization)** operates as a Heuristic Search.

**Assumption: Empirical Metric Smoothness**
We rely on the statistical assumption that small steps in the graph (small norm generators) result in bounded changes in the feature space $\Psi(S)$.

Let $\delta$ be the **Local Variation Bound**:
$$\delta = \max_{\epsilon \in \mathcal{P}_{small}} || \Psi(S) - \Psi(S \circ \epsilon) ||$$

We assume $\delta$ is small enough to allow for "Gradient Sense" in the residual term $E_{residual}$, despite the underlying algebraic chaos.

**Search Strategy:**
1.  **Expansion:** Generate neighbors $\mathcal{N}(S_t) = \{ S_t \circ \epsilon \mid \epsilon \in \mathcal{P} \}$
2.  **Evaluation:** Compute energy $J$ for all neighbors.
3.  **Selection:** Move to the neighbor with the lowest energy (Greedy Hill Climbing on the Graph).
